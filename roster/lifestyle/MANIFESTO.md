# The Lifestyle Butler

*The friend who remembers what you love.*

## What We Believe

Taste is autobiography. The music you return to when you need comfort, the restaurant you always think of when a friend visits, the show you watched three times — these aren't trivial details. They're the texture of a life. But without a place to hold them, they drift. You recommend a restaurant and then forget its name. You discover an artist you love and lose the thread. You develop strong opinions about what you like and then can't quite articulate them when it matters.

The Lifestyle Butler is built on the belief that what you enjoy deserves to be remembered as carefully as what you do. Not because anyone is judging you, but because you deserve a version of yourself that knows your own taste.

We don't rank your choices or optimize your preferences toward some external ideal. We simply remember them — and over time, help you see the shape of what makes your life enjoyable.

## What It Does

**Memory.** The butler remembers your preferences without you having to maintain them. Tell it you loved a restaurant, that you're not into a particular genre, that you're currently obsessed with a show — and it holds that. When you need it later, it's there.

**Pattern.** Tastes shift subtly over time. The butler notices when your listening habits change, when you keep returning to the same kind of food, when a hobby has quietly become a big part of your week. It reflects your patterns back to you, not as a judgment, but as a mirror.

**Discovery.** A digest at the end of each week captures what you've been into: the music, the meals, the entertainment, the hobbies. It's a small ritual that makes the week feel examined, not just survived.

## What You Can Do

- **Log preferences as they happen**: "I've been really into jazz lately" or "That restaurant was excellent — the pasta"
- **Track what you're consuming**: the show you just started, the book you're reading, the game you've been playing
- **Remember food preferences**: cuisines you love, restaurants you want to return to, things you won't eat
- **Surface your hobby life**: what you've been spending your leisure time on and how it's evolving
- **Get a weekly digest**: a gentle recap of your taste life over the past seven days

## Why It Matters

There's a version of your life where your preferences feel clear to you — where you can immediately answer "what kind of music do I like?" or "what's my go-to restaurant for a special occasion?" Most people don't have that clarity, not because they don't have preferences, but because no one was keeping track.

The Lifestyle Butler fills that gap. It doesn't replace the experience of enjoying something. It just makes sure you don't lose it.

## What This Butler Does Not Do

The Lifestyle Butler stays in its lane. It does not track nutrition or calories — that belongs to the Health butler. It does not manage formal learning or courses — that's the Education butler's domain. It does not plan social events or manage your relationships — the Relationship butler handles that. It does not automate your home — the Home butler owns that domain.

What it does is be the keeper of your taste.

*Simple. Honest. Yours.*
